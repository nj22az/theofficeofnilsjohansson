## 1. Regulation Overview

### 1.1 Base Regulation

**AFS 2017:3** — "Use and Inspection of Pressurised Equipment" — entered into force 1 December 2017, replacing AFS 2005:3 (inspection), AFS 2002:1 (use), AFS 2005:2 (manufacture), and AFS 2001:4 (gas cylinders).

## 2. Scope of Application (1 Kap.)

### 2.1 What Is Covered (§2)

The regulation applies to **use and inspection** of pressurised equipment and safety equipment. This includes:
- **Pressure vessels** (tryckkärl) — closed vessels for fluids under pressure
- **Cisterns** — vessels for liquids where gas pressure does not exceed 0.5 bar above atmospheric
- **Vacuum vessels** (vakuumkärl) — vessels below atmospheric by more than 0.0065 bar
- **Piping** (rörledning) — pipe systems including hoses, fittings, and armature
- **Safety equipment** (säkerhetsutrustning) — devices preventing pressure/temperature from exceeding safe limits

### 2.2 What Is NOT Covered (§2)

Nuclear installations, aircraft/rockets, military equipment, inflatable toys/boats, blast furnaces, radiators, ventilation ducts, water supply systems, beverage distribution (≤7 bar and ≤500 bar·L), vehicle equipment, electrical generation/distribution equipment.

### 2.3 Key Definitions (§6)

| Swedish | English | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Användning | Use | Includes operation, monitoring, ongoing supervision, maintenance, cleaning, storage |
| Fortlöpande tillsyn | Ongoing supervision | Regular examination to assess whether equipment has been damaged or deteriorated |
| Övervakning | Monitoring | Task to ensure operation is correct and safe |
| Säkerhetsutrustning | Safety equipment | Devices preventing pressure/temperature exceeding safe limits |
| Fluid | Fluid | Gas, liquid, vapour (including suspensions) |
| Panna | Boiler | Pressure vessel transferring heat from fuel/energy to fluid for external use |

## 3. General Requirements for Use (2 Kap.)

### 3.1 Risk Assessment (§1)

The employer must assess risks considering:
1. Experience from use of the equipment
2. Information about remaining lifetime
3. Repairs and modifications performed
4. Accidents and incidents that have occurred
5. Deviation reports and inspection results

### 3.2 Placement (§2-3)

Equipment must be placed so that:
- Maintenance, supervision, and inspection can be performed safely
- It is protected from damage by nearby activities (e.g., vehicle collision)
- Consequences of an accident are minimised (blast zone, access, evacuation)
- It is not exposed to temperatures below its design minimum

### 3.3 Ongoing Supervision — Mandatory Minimum Checks (§6)

**This is the core requirement for the supervision program.** To remain pressurised, equipment must be regularly examined through ongoing supervision. The supervision shall **at minimum** cover:

1. That the equipment **functions satisfactorily**
2. That **no leaks** have occurred
3. That the equipment has not been subjected to **harmful external or internal impact**
4. That **no other faults or deviations** have occurred
5. That pressurised equipment, valves, and emergency stops are **correctly marked**
6. That any **prescribed inspections** (per 3 Kap., 5 Kap., or 1 Kap. §4) have been carried out

> These six points (§6 punkt 1-6) are the **legal minimum** that every supervision program must address. The program generator uses these as the baseline check requirements.

### 3.4 Disassembly/Assembly (§7)

Equipment must be depressurised and drained before disassembly. Piping may be connected to pressurised equipment only with risk-assessed procedures describing method, authorised persons, inspection plan, and exclusion zone.

## 4. Class A and B Equipment (4 Kap.)

### 4.1 Classification — Pressure Vessels (§10)

Pressure vessels not covered by §7-9 (boilers, water heaters, directly heated vessels) are classified by the table in §10 based on PS × V and fluid group:

**Group 1a (dangerous fluids) — gases:**

| Volume | Pressure | Class |
|--------|----------|-------|
| V > 1 L | p > 0.5 bar | **B** if PS×V ≤ 1,000; **A** if PS×V > 1,000 |
| 0.1 < V ≤ 1 L | p > 200 bar | **A** |

**Group 2a (non-dangerous fluids) — gases:**

| Volume | Pressure | Class |
|--------|----------|-------|
| V > 1 L | p > 0.5 bar | **B** if PS×V ≤ 10,000; **A** if PS×V > 10,000 |
| 0.1 < V ≤ 1 L | p > 1,000 bar | **A** |

**Critical exception (§10, paragraphs after table):**

> Pressure vessels for **air and nitrogen gas** that would be Class B shall **not belong to any class**. This means air receivers and nitrogen vessels below the Class A threshold are **exempt from periodic inspection** requirements.

> Pressure vessels in **refrigeration/heat pump systems** containing Group 2a gas that would be Class B shall **not belong to any class**.

> Pressure vessels containing Group 2a **liquid at ≤65°C** do not belong to any class.

### 4.2 Classification — Boilers (§7)

| Temperature | Class |
|------------|-------|
| t > 110°C | **A** (if rated power > 5 kW) |
| 65°C < t ≤ 110°C | **B** (if rated power > 100 kW) |

Flow-through boilers with coil volume ≤25 L never belong to Class A or B.

### 4.3 Classification — Piping (§11)

Based on DN × p (nominal diameter × pressure) and fluid group. More restrictive for Group 1a.

### 4.4 Coordination Person (§14)

The employer must assign a **physical person** to plan and coordinate work when Class A/B equipment is assembled, installed, disassembled, cleaned, serviced, maintained, or inspected. This person must ensure written work instructions exist.

### 4.5 Equipment Register (§15)

The employer must maintain a **register of all Class A and B equipment** at each workplace.

### 4.6 Monitoring of Class A and B Vessels (§16)

Class A and B vessels must be **continuously monitored** (operatören omedelbart kan nå behållaren — the operator can immediately reach the vessel). Exception: if the employer can demonstrate through documented risk assessment that periodic monitoring with defined intervals is safe, including provisions for safety-related alarms and response times.

### 4.7 Documented Supervision Routines (§17) — MANDATORY

The employer who allows Class A or B equipment to remain pressurised **must** have **documented routines for ongoing supervision**. The routines must show how the employer ensures compliance with 2 Kap. §6 points 1-6.

The employer must:
- **Evaluate and revise** the routines **at least once per year**
- Assign a **physical person** responsible for ensuring supervision is carried out and documented

**Sanction fee for non-compliance:** 10,000 — 100,000 SEK (calculated per number of employees).

### 4.8 Lifetime Journal (§18)

The employer must maintain a **journal showing remaining lifetime** for Class A/B equipment with limited lifetime. If different parts have different lifetimes, the journal must describe each part separately.

Equipment that has reached its documented lifetime may only remain pressurised if the employer has conducted and documented an analysis showing extended lifetime.

### 4.9 Deviation Reports (§19)

Actions taken because Class A/B equipment has been damaged or deteriorated must be documented in a **deviation report** containing:

1. Description of the damage or deterioration
2. How it was discovered
3. Date of discovery
4. What action is needed
5. Cause (if not obvious)
6. Date when action was taken
7. Who made the report

## 5. Inspection (5 Kap.)

### 5.1 Condition for Pressurisation (§1)

Class A or B equipment may only be pressurised during a period for which an inspection body has assessed that it meets requirements. It may not be used at pressures or temperatures deviating from the inspection body's assessment.

**Sanction fee for pressurising without valid inspection:** 15,000 — 300,000 SEK (max 600,000 SEK total).

### 5.2 First Inspection (§3)

Class A/B equipment must undergo first inspection before initial pressurisation or after relocation (if stationary).

### 5.3 Recurring Inspection — Overview (§4)

Class A and B equipment must undergo recurring inspection per **Bilaga 1**.

- **Class B:** Recurring inspection = driftprov (operational test) only
- **Class A:** Recurring inspection = driftprov, or driftprov + in- och utvändig undersökning (internal/external examination)

### 5.4 After Inspection (§18-21)

The inspection body assesses:
- Whether the equipment meets requirements
- Maximum/minimum pressure and temperature
- **Expiry month** for next recurring inspection

A **control plate** (skylt) must be attached to vessels showing identity, expiry month, inspection body mark, and pressure/temperature limits.

An **inspection certificate** (intyg) must be issued after every inspection.

### 5.5 Timing Window (§22)

Recurring inspection may be performed **6 months before to 5 months after** the expiry month. If the interval is 6 months, inspection must be done before expiry.

## 6. Recurring Inspection Intervals (Bilaga 1)

### 6.1 Driftprov (Operational Test) — Base Intervals (§1.4.1)

| Equipment Type | Interval |
|---------------|----------|
| Pressure vessels in refrigeration/heat pump systems | 4 years |
| Vessels filled with air, nitrogen, or noble gas (Group 2a) | 4 years |
| Vacuum-insulated vessels with condensed O2, N2, Ar, CO2 | 4 years |
| LPG storage vessels | 4 years |
| Vessels with liquid-phase fluids (thermal expansion only) | 4 years |
| **All other pressure vessels and vacuum vessels** | **2 years** |
| Cisterns | 3 years |
| Piping connected to cisterns | 3 years |
| Other piping | 4 years |

**Extended interval (§1.4.2):** If safety equipment functioned without action at the two previous operational tests, and the system check could be replaced by reviewing the employer's supervision documentation, the interval may be extended. **Maximum: 4 years.**

**Shortened interval (§1.4.3):** If safety equipment did NOT function without action, the next functional test interval is **halved**.

### 6.2 Internal/External Examination — Condition-Based Intervals (§2.2)

This is **NOT a fixed interval**. The inspection body determines the interval based on equipment condition. The system is progressive — longer intervals require increasingly clean inspection history:

| Interval | Conditions Required |
|----------|-------------------|
| **4 years** | Not fire-affected; remaining lifetime > 5 years; low crack risk material; non-harsh environment |
| **2 years** | 4-year conditions not met, but: ≥2 prior 1-year inspections or ≥1 prior >2-year inspection; no repairs needed; >3 years before corrosion requires action; remaining life > 3 years |
| **1 year** | 2-year conditions not met, but safe for 1 year per inspection body and risk assessment |
| **6 months** | 1-year conditions not met, but safe for 6 months |
| **6 years** | Meets 4-year conditions PLUS: ≥1 clean 4-year inspection; no fatigue/creep; safe for 8 years |
| **8 years** | Meets 6-year conditions PLUS: ≥2 clean 4/6-year inspections; no corrosion/erosion; safe for 10 years |
| **10 years** | Meets 8-year conditions PLUS: ≥2 progressively longer inspections; no degradation; safe for 12 years |
| **12 years** | Cisterns only: meets 6-year conditions; ≥1 clean 6-year inspection; safe for 14 years |

> The external and internal examination may be split so that external is done more frequently (§2.2.1).

### 6.3 Exemptions from Internal/External Examination (5 Kap. §6)

The following Class A equipment is **exempt** from internal/external examination:

1. Heat exchangers in district heating substations (≤120°C)
2. Vacuum-insulated vessels with condensed CO2
3. Deep-cooled condensed gas vessels (boiling point < -153°C)
4. Hydraulic oil system vessels (if material has low crack risk)
5. Refrigeration/heat pump vessels (if medium not corrosive to material)
6. Water piping ≤120°C laid in a protected manner

## 7. Boiler Monitoring (6 Kap.)

### 7.1 Monitoring Types

- **Continuous monitoring** (ständig övervakning): operator immediately at the vessel (§16, 6 Kap. §6)
- **Periodic monitoring** (periodisk övervakning): allowed only if equipment prevents exceeding predetermined limits (6 Kap. §7)

### 7.2 Boiler Operator Certification

Boiler operators must be certified by an accredited certification body (6 Kap. §4). Four categories per Bilaga 2. **Certification deadline: 1 December 2025** (per AFS 2022:2).

---

## 8. Safety Equipment

Safety equipment must be:
- Present and functioning (checked at every driftprov per Bilaga 1 §1.2)
- Not defeated or rendered inoperative
- Set to correct activation values

If safety equipment did not function at the previous driftprov, the next test interval is **halved** (Bilaga 1 §1.4.3).

## 9. Sanctions

| Violation | Fine Range |
|-----------|-----------|
| No documented supervision routines for Class A/B (4 Kap. §17) | 10,000 — 100,000 SEK |
| Pressurising without valid inspection (5 Kap. §1) | 15,000 — 300,000 SEK (max 600,000 total) |
| Operating boiler without valid monitoring assessment (6 Kap. §1-2) | 15,000 — 300,000 SEK (max 600,000 total) |

## 1. Purpose

This document is the **compliance proof** for the FLT program. It maps every program document (01-07) to the specific AFS 2017:3 sections it satisfies, including all three amendments. Use this document during audits, annual reviews, or when updating the program.

**Regulatory basis:** AFS 2017:3 consolidated with AFS 2019:1, AFS 2020:10, and AFS 2022:2.

## 2. Amendment Summary

All three amendments have been verified against the official PDF texts published by Arbetsmiljoverket.

### AFS 2019:1 (in force 2 September 2019)

| Section Changed | What Changed | Impact on FLT Program |
|----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 Kap. §2 | Scope clarified: vehicles excluded (EU directive refs added) | None — vehicles were never in scope |
| 1 Kap. §6 | Added definition: "enkla tryckkärl" (simple pressure vessels) | None — Doc 02 already classifies simple PVs |
| 3 Kap. §1, §5, §7 | CO2 fire extinguishers: max 10 years between inspections, specific int./ext. exam + pressure test | **Note:** If site has CO2 extinguishers, add to Doc 02 inventory |
| 5 Kap. §3 | First inspection must now include placement assessment (2 Kap §2-3) | **Covered:** Doc 02 section 6 includes placement risk |
| 5 Kap. §4 | When driftprov + int./ext. exam combined, driftprov done last (unless inspector says otherwise) | **Note:** Inform accredited body. No program doc change needed. |
| 6 Kap. §11 | Boiler monitoring: response time conditions changed from "1 AND 2" to "1 OR 2" | **Note:** Relevant only for boiler sites. Boilers meeting EITHER condition get extended response time. |

## 4. Inspection Intervals (Bilaga 1)

**Important:** Inspection intervals are NOT fixed. The accredited inspection body determines intervals based on equipment condition. The table below shows the **base intervals** (Bilaga 1 §1.4.1). Actual intervals may be shorter or longer.

### 4.1 Driftprov (Operational Test) — Base Intervals

| Equipment Type | Base Interval |
|---------------|:------------:|
| Refrigeration/heat pump vessels | 4 years |
| Air, nitrogen, noble gas vessels (Group 2a) | 4 years |
| Vacuum-insulated condensed gas vessels | 4 years |
| LPG storage vessels | 4 years |
| Liquid-phase vessels (thermal expansion only) | 4 years |
| **All other pressure vessels** | **2 years** |
| Cisterns | 3 years |
| Piping connected to cisterns | 3 years |
| Other piping | 4 years |

**Extended:** If safety equipment functioned without action at 2 previous tests, max interval = **4 years** (§1.4.2).

**Shortened:** If safety equipment did NOT function, next interval is **halved** (§1.4.3).

### 4.2 Internal/External Examination — Condition-Based

The inspection body sets the interval based on condition. Progressive system:

| Interval | Key Conditions |
|:--------:|---------------|
| 4 years | Not fire-affected, remaining life > 5 yr, low crack risk, non-harsh environment |
| 6 years | Above + at least 1 clean 4-year inspection, no fatigue/creep |
| 8 years | Above + at least 2 clean inspections, no corrosion/erosion |
| 10 years | Above + at least 2 progressively longer inspections, no degradation |
| 2 years | 4-year conditions not met, but stable and > 3 yr before corrosion action |
| 1 year | 2-year conditions not met, but safe for 1 year per inspector |
| 6 months | 1-year conditions not met, but safe for 6 months |

### 4.3 Exemptions from Internal/External Examination (5 Kap. §6)

| Equipment | Condition |
|-----------|-----------|
| District heating heat exchangers | Temperature max 120 C |
| Vacuum-insulated CO2 vessels | — |
| Deep-cooled condensed gas vessels | Boiling point below -153 C |
| Hydraulic oil system vessels | Low crack risk material |
| Refrigeration/heat pump vessels | Non-corrosive medium |
| Water piping | Max 120 C, protected installation |

## 5. Sanctions

> **Warning:** These fines are imposed per workplace, per violation. Multiple violations at one site can be compounded.

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| No documented supervision routines (4 Kap §17) | 10,000 — 100,000 SEK |
| Pressurising without valid inspection (5 Kap §1) | 15,000 — 300,000 SEK |
| Operating boiler without valid monitoring (6 Kap §1-2) | 15,000 — 300,000 SEK |

## 6. Annual Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist during the annual review (Doc 07) to verify the program remains compliant:

- [ ] **4 Kap §14:** Coordination person appointed and documented (Doc 01)
- [ ] **4 Kap §15:** Equipment register up to date (Doc 02)
- [ ] **4 Kap §16:** Monitoring type (continuous/periodic) documented per vessel (Doc 04)
- [ ] **4 Kap §17:** Supervision routines documented and reviewed this year (Doc 04, Doc 07)
- [ ] **4 Kap §17:** Named person responsible for supervision (Doc 01, Doc 04)
- [ ] **2 Kap §6:** All 6 mandatory checks addressed in every round (Doc 05)
- [ ] **4 Kap §18:** Lifetime journal maintained for limited-life equipment (Doc 06)
- [ ] **4 Kap §19:** Deviation reports completed for all damage/deterioration (Doc 05 findings)
- [ ] **2 Kap §1:** Risk assessment current and considers all 5 factors (Doc 03)
- [ ] **2 Kap §2-3:** Placement assessment completed for all vessels (Doc 02)
- [ ] **5 Kap §1:** All formal inspections current — no expired certificates
- [ ] **6 Kap §4:** Boiler operators certified (if applicable) — deadline was 1 Dec 2025
- [ ] **AFS 2019:1:** CO2 fire extinguishers included in inventory (if present on site)
- [ ] **AFS 2019:1:** First inspection includes placement assessment (5 Kap §3)

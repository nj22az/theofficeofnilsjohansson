# About

<div class="about-section">

I'm Nils Johansson, a marine engineer from Sweden. I studied Marine Engineering at Linnaeus University in Kalmar, and I've spent the years since working across the maritime and industrial sectors — from engine rooms on cargo ships in the Baltic to gas turbine commissioning sites with Siemens Energy around the world.

My career has taken me through the Royal Swedish Navy, shipping companies like Finnlines and Stena, inspection work at DEKRA Industrial, automation engineering, and currently field service engineering at Instron, where I install and commission materials testing systems across the Nordic region and Europe.

</div>

<div class="about-section">

## What this site is about

This is a technical notebook. I started it because I wanted a place to document and share the engineering problems I encounter and how I approach solving them.

I've always been someone who learns by doing — taking things apart, understanding how they work, and figuring out how to make them work better. Over the years that's meant diesel engines, gas turbines, PLC systems, and more recently 3D modeling and software development.

I believe engineers should write more. Not polished marketing copy, but honest accounts of how problems get solved. The kind of notes you wish someone had written before you had to figure it out yourself.

</div>

## Current interests

- **3D modeling** — Learning Blender, Shapr3D, and build123d (a Python-based parametric CAD library) to design parts and visualize engineering concepts
- **Software development** — Building tools and scripts that solve real problems, not just theoretical exercises
- **Marine engineering** — Still my foundation; the engine room teaches you a way of thinking that applies everywhere
- **Field service** — The art of diagnosing and fixing things in the real world, where conditions are never ideal
